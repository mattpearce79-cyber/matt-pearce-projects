Decision 01
Intent-based taxonomy
12+ mixed items reorganised into intent-based categories: Browse by age and Browse by subject serve teachers finding resources; Leaders and Parents serve distinct non-teacher audiences with their own product hubs; Twinkl Store is isolated as a transactional destination. Each item maps to a single user intent rather than mixing audience types, age bands, and commercial destinations in one flat list.
Decision 02
Curriculum stages over age bands
The age dropdown uses UK curriculum stages (Early Years, KS1, KS2, KS3, KS4-5) as column headers, with age ranges shown as secondary labels. Teachers think in key stages, not raw age numbers.
Decision 03
Split button: two explicit click targets
The label text links to the category landing page on click. The chevron opens the mega menu on click. No hover dependency, no delay, no ambiguity. Separate hover states on each target reinforce that the two elements do different things.
Decision 04
Single primary CTA
Join for FREE is the only filled button in the header. Sign In, School Enquiries and the language selector are grouped as secondary utility links. The trial button is removed from the nav entirely and surfaced post-login.
Decision 05
Disney as distinct featured partner
Disney uses a pill with a background and border that is visually different from both the resting and hover states of standard nav items. It leads the nav row to signal prominence without polluting the primary taxonomy.
Decision 06
Two-panel mega menu: stage selector + stage-specific content
Each stage has a content panel matched to its curriculum context. EYFS shows Areas of Learning and Themes; KS1 and KS2 promote English and Maths as dedicated columns alongside Subjects and Planning; KS3 and KS4-5 switch to subject-specific columns (English, Maths, Science, More Subjects), reflecting secondary browsing patterns. Twinkl Recommends is persistent across all stages as an editorial anchor.
