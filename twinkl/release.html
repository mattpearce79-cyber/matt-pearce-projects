Post-launch metrics

How I would measure success at scale.

01

Task completion rate

The primary success signal. Can a teacher find what they need in fewer steps and less time? Measured through moderated usability testing with a representative sample of educators before and after release.

02

Nav vs search ratio

If the redesign is working, fewer users should need to fall back to search out of frustration. A shift toward nav-initiated journeys indicates the structure is matching how teachers think.

03

Time on task

Reduction in mean time and variance across common journeys such as finding a KS2 maths worksheet. Low variance is as important as low mean — it indicates the design works consistently across different users, not just some of them.

04

Conversion rate

Do users who successfully navigate to a resource convert to paid subscriptions at a higher rate than those who drop off mid-journey? A nav that demonstrates Twinkl’s value faster should show up in acquisition data.

What I would test next

Recommended next steps to validate and iterate.

01

Usability testing at scale

Moderated usability testing with a larger sample of classroom teachers across different Key Stages and regions.

02

A/B test the interaction model

A/B test the click-only mega menu trigger against the current hover behaviour, measuring task completion and time on task.

03

Card sorting

Card sorting exercise with real educators to validate the proposed taxonomy — do Browse by age, Browse by subject and Teachers match how teachers mentally organise their work?

04

Accessibility audit

Full WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility audit against the redesigned components before development handover.

05

Post-launch monitoring

Monitor nav vs search usage ratio and conversion rate in the first 30 days post-launch to establish a baseline for iteration.
